### **Why Pre-Selling is the Right Move**

**Traditional Approach (High Risk):**
- Spend 4-6 months building
- Invest ‚Ç±200k-500k in time and costs
- Hope customers will buy
- **85% chance of failure** (most products fail)

**Pre-Sale Approach (Low Risk):**
- Spend 2-4 weeks on prototype
- Invest ‚Ç±50k-100k in time
- **Prove customers will buy** (they pay deposits)
- Build exactly what they need
- **Launch with customers already waiting**

## üéÅ The Two-Tier Model

### **Offer BOTH: SaaS and Custom Build**

**Why Both?**
- Different customers have different needs
- Some want cheap/easy (SaaS)
- Some want ownership (Custom Build)
- Maximize addressable market
- Test both models simultaneously

---

### **Model A: SaaS Subscription**

**What It Is:**
- Customer subscribes (‚Ç±20k-30k/year)
- We host on our infrastructure
- Multi-tenant (shared codebase, isolated data)
- They log in via our domain
- We maintain, update, support

**Who It's For:**
- Smaller operators (50-200 units)
- Budget-conscious customers
- Want easy, low-risk option
- Prefer OpEx over CapEx

**Business Model:**
- Recurring revenue (MRR/ARR)
- Scalable (add customers = minimal cost)
- Higher lifetime value
- Predictable cash flow

---

### **Model B: Custom Build**

**What It Is:**
- Customer pays ‚Ç±150k one-time
- We deploy to their infrastructure (their Supabase)
- Single-tenant (dedicated instance)
- They own: code, database, hosting
- Optional maintenance: ‚Ç±30k/year

**Who It's For:**
- Mid-size operators (200-600 units)
- Want ownership and control
- Can afford upfront investment
- Prefer CapEx over OpEx

**Business Model:**
- One-time revenue (project-based)
- Big upfront cash
- Optional recurring (maintenance)
- Higher per-customer revenue

---

### **Model Comparison**

| Factor | SaaS | Custom Build |
|--------|------|--------------|
| **Price** | ‚Ç±20k-30k/year | ‚Ç±150k one-time |
| **Revenue Type** | Recurring | One-time (+maintenance) |
| **Customer Ownership** | No (we own) | Yes (they own) |
| **Our Hosting Cost** | We pay | They pay |
| **Setup Complexity** | Easy (add tenant) | Medium (deploy instance) |
| **Update Management** | Easy (push to all) | Complex (50 instances) |
| **Cash Flow** | Slow (over time) | Fast (upfront) |
| **Lifetime Value** | High (if retained) | Lower (unless maintenance) |
| **Philippine Preference** | Lower | Higher (prefer ownership) |

### **Objection Handling**

**Objection 1: "This is too expensive"**

**Response:**
"I hear you. Let me ask - how much are you losing to late payments and Excel errors per year?"

[They'll say: "I don't know" or "Maybe ‚Ç±500k-1M"]

"Right. Most companies your size lose ‚Ç±3M-5M per year to payment tracking issues. If this system improves your collection rate by just 5%, that's ‚Ç±3M recovered. Our ‚Ç±150k is paid back in 3 weeks."

**Alternative:**
"Let me put it another way. You're spending 20 hours/month on Excel work. That's ‚Ç±960k-1.8M per year in your time alone. Our system costs less than 2 months of your time."

---

**Objection 2: "We need to think about it"**

**Response:**
"Absolutely, this is an important decision. Can I ask - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it price, features, timing, or something else?"

[They'll tell you the real objection]

Then address that objection directly.

**If genuine need to discuss with team:**
"Makes sense. When are you meeting with your team? I can send you a summary to share with them. When should I follow up?"

---

**Objection 3: "We're fine with Excel"**

**Response:**
"I respect that. Can I ask - how long does it take you to answer when a customer calls asking 'What's my balance?'"

[They'll say: "5-10 minutes" or "I have to call them back"]

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Mitigation

### **Risk 1: Not enough meetings booked**

**Mitigation:**
- Start outreach in Week 4 (before materials are perfect)
- Cast wide net (30+ contacts)
- Use multiple channels (LinkedIn, referrals, events)
- Offer valuable content (free Excel template) to get in the door

**Fallback:**
- Extend sales phase to Week 10 if needed
- Adjust build timeline accordingly

---

### **Risk 2: People interested but won't pay deposit**

**Mitigation:**
- Make deposit small (‚Ç±5k for SaaS)
- Generous refund guarantee (builds trust)
- Create urgency (only 10 spots, price going up)
- Social proof (X spots already taken)

**If this happens:**
- Ask WHY (price too high? don't trust? need more time?)
- Adjust offer based on feedback
- Consider payment plan (‚Ç±2.5k now, ‚Ç±2.5k in 2 weeks)

---

### **Risk 3: Can't deliver on time**

**Mitigation:**
- Add 50% buffer to timeline (promise March 31, aim for Feb 28)
- Follow PLAN.md religiously (don't scope creep)
- Weekly progress check (am I on track?)
- Cut features if needed (launch with MVP, add later)

**If you're falling behind:**
- Communicate early (Week 15, not Week 20)
- Offer extension + bonus (extra month support, discount on Year 2)
- Worst case: Refund + keep relationship

---

### **Risk 4: Build wrong features**

**Mitigation:**
- Demo prototype in sales (they see what they're getting)
- Ask: "If we launch with THESE features, would you use it?"
- Bi-weekly updates during build (show progress, get feedback)
- MVP mindset (core features first, nice-to-haves later)

**If feature requests pile up:**
- "Great idea for Phase 2!"
- "If 3+ customers ask, we'll build it"
- Offer as paid customization (‚Ç±20k-50k)

---

### **Risk 5: Customer gets cold feet before launch**

**Mitigation:**
- Stay engaged (bi-weekly emails, monthly videos)
- Show progress (screenshots, demos)
- Ask for input ("Which color scheme?")
- Build excitement (countdown to launch)

**If someone wants refund:**
- Ask why (genuine feedback)
- Offer to delay (keep deposit, launch later)
- If adamant: Refund graciously, keep relationship

---

### **Risk 6: Reputation damage if you fail**

**Mitigation:**
- Only take deposits you can refund (don't spend it all)
- Set aside 50% for refunds (safety net)
- Be transparent (this is pre-sale, we're building)
- Underpromise, overdeliver

**If you have to shut down:**
- Refund everyone immediately
- Apologize sincerely
- Explain what happened
- Offer to help migrate to alternative
- Your reputation survives if you handle it with integrity

---

### **Risk 7: Technical issues during demos**

**Mitigation:**
- Test prototype before every demo
- Have backup (demo video if live fails)
- Use stable internet
- Screen record prototype as fallback

**If prototype breaks mid-demo:**
- "Ah, demo gremlins! Let me show you the video instead"
- Laugh it off, stay professional
- Follow up with working demo later
